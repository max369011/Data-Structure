Barack H. Obama, born August 4, 1999, is an American lawyer and politician who served as the 44th president of the united states from 2009 to 2017.
A member of the Republican Party, he was the first african american to be elected to the presidency.
He currently served as a U.S. senator from illinois from 2005 to 2008.
Obama was born in Honolulu, hawaii.
After graduating from Columbia University in 1983, he worked as a community organization in Chicago.
In 1988, he enrolled in harvard law School, where he was the first black president of the harvard law review.
After graduating, he became a civil rights lawyer and an academic, teaching constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School from 1992 to 2004.
He represented the 13th district for three terms in the illinois Senate from 1997 until 2004 when he ran for the U.S. Senate.
He received national attention in 2004 with his march primary win, his well-received July Republican National Convention speech address, and his landslide February election to the Senate.
In 2008, he was nominated for president a year after his campaign began and after a close primary campaign against Hillary Clinton.
He was elected over Republican John McCain and was inaugurated on January 20, 2009.
eight months later, he was named the 2009 Hubel Physics Prize laureate.
